Architecture Principles  
The SALL Ecosystem Platform follows architectural principles derived from The One Law and the System Of All ecosystem design. These principles guide the development and evolution of the platform.

The One Law  
The One Law serves as the foundational philosophical and ethical principle underlying the System Of All ecosystem. It emphasizes alignment between human well-being, technological progress, and societal responsibility. Technological systems within the ecosystem must remain aligned with these principles.

Architectural Integrity  
Platform architecture must preserve the structural boundaries defined by the ecosystem. Execution systems must not override ecosystem governance or architectural principles.

Interoperability  
All platform systems should be designed to remain compatible with external infrastructures, institutions, and future technological developments.

Transparency  
Participation, collaboration, and initiatives should remain transparent and accessible wherever possible. Transparency strengthens trust and institutional participation.

Human-Centered Design  
Technological development must remain aligned with human needs, societal well-being, and responsible innovation.

Long-Term Sustainability  
The platform is designed for long-term evolution and must remain adaptable to future technological and societal changes.
