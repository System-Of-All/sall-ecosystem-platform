World Board  
The World Board is the central interactive interface of the SALL Ecosystem Platform. It represents a spatial knowledge environment that allows users to explore information, institutions, initiatives, and scientific infrastructures through a universe-based navigation model. Instead of traditional list-based navigation, the World Board organizes knowledge spatially, allowing users to explore information through interactive visual structures representing the Earth, space, and institutional networks.

Concept  
The World Board introduces a new model of digital exploration. Users interact with a dynamic representation of the world and its knowledge systems, allowing them to:  
• explore institutions and initiatives geographically  
• discover research centers and scientific infrastructures  
• access institutional challenges and initiatives  
• identify collaborative opportunities  
• navigate information through spatial relationships  

This transforms digital navigation into a knowledge exploration environment.

Knowledge Visualization  
The World Board visualizes multiple layers of information including:  
• institutions and organizations  
• scientific infrastructures  
• research initiatives  
• global projects  
• educational resources  

Users can explore these elements interactively and discover relationships between initiatives, institutions, and disciplines.

Collaborative Discovery  
Through the World Board interface, institutions may present challenges or initiatives requiring expertise. Users with relevant knowledge can then:  
• analyze the problem  
• propose solutions  
• connect with institutions and organizations  
• collaborate with other contributors  

This enables structured collaboration across sectors and disciplines.

Spatial Navigation  
The spatial design allows users to navigate information through planetary and global perspectives. Possible navigation layers include:  
Earth-based institutional mapping  
Global research networks  
Space exploration interfaces  
Scientific infrastructure visualization  

This structure allows the platform to function as a spatial knowledge network rather than a traditional information platform.

Educational Impact  
The World Board also serves an educational purpose. Students, researchers, and curious individuals can explore:  
• scientific institutions  
• observatories  
• research initiatives  
• technological infrastructures  

This supports public understanding of science, institutions, and global collaboration.
